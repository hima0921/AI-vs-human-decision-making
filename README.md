# Cultural Differences in Trust: AI vs Human Decision-Making

## Overview
This project explores how people make decisions when AI and human expert recommendations conflict. In particular, it looks at whether individuals rely more on perceived competence or perceived legitimacy (authority), and whether this differs across cultural contexts (United States vs. Mexico).

## Research Focus
The main question is:
- Do people prefer AI or human advice when the two disagree?
- Is this decision driven more by who seems more *accurate* (competence) or who has more *authority* (legitimacy)?
- Does this pattern change depending on cultural background?

## Study Design
- Cross-cultural comparison: United States vs. Mexico  
- Within-subject design with multiple decision scenarios  
- Domains include both technical (e.g., medical, financial) and interpersonal (e.g., mental health, career) contexts  
- Measures include advisor choice, confidence, perceived competence, and perceived legitimacy  

## Analysis Approach
- Mixed-effects logistic regression for decision outcomes  
- Linear mixed-effects models for perception measures  
- Focus on how legitimacy and competence predict choices across cultural groups
